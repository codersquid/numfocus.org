Legal advice on developer contract

Tony Sebro’s answer to the question regarding developers, such as Jonathan Frederic, needing a contract.

Recap of 3/26 call with Tony Sebro regarding NumFocus hiring developers as contractors:

  1. Developers working for a project should have an Independent Contractor relationship with NumFocus.
  2. A signed agreement is needed and the developer should be given a proposal, from NF of work that is to be done.
  3. The agreement should be separate from the proposal, and contain deliverables and the process in which the work will be evaluated. It should state that the work is licensed under BSD and make sure that NF retains ownership or that the copyright is assigned to NF. [Unless NF wants the contractor to own copyrights]
  4. It’s just good practice to make sure it’s the project’s work and not the individual’s.
  5. Tony has a template of an agreement we can use. He would not have to create a new document. He could customize the template in less than one hour.
  6. He highly recommends the contractor post the deliverables in a public place, e.g. IPython’s blog or website, saying what was done, how long was spent and that an invoice was given to NF.
  7. “In case of an audit you can’t point the IRS to commit logs.”
  8. He also suggested that to “clean things up” NF should own all of the project’s domains.

Leah will ask Tony to contact Fernando (who forwards to the IPython FSA co-signer mailing list) about customizing the template for IPython.
